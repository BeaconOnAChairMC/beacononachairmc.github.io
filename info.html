Some people might call this a horrible website. Some people might criticize it.



Maybe they want to see a website they've always been seeing, the ones with the fancy CSS and the fancy Javascript.


Well I'm not here to give you any of that bullsh*t. I'm here to give you text.

Lots and lots of it.


Sometimes we see a website, and go "Oh wow! That is such a great website!"

That would be the normal reaction to something that looks great.

Sometimes, however, we forget that the website is slow, cluttered and doesn't do the thing you want it to do.

What's the point of a website if the design is overshadowing the content?

What's the point of a website if the huge, fancy buttons are limiting the functionality of the website?



Well, not anymore. This website only uses paragraphs.

There isn't a single line of CSS, Javascript or even divs in this website.

This provides you with exactly what you wanted from it: something to read.

Of course, if you didn't want to read anything, then just leave. Go.

If you want to read something, stay I guess.





A website is like a piece of land to me. Or a building. Or a house.

In this vast space on the internet, I get to own something. I get to have something that I can claim is mine (if I remember to pay, of course).

If I were running some business or something, I'd probably want this website to be more user-friendly.

I'd probably make it more attractive, and more functional.

Unfortunately for those of you who are reading this, I'm not running some business.

This website is mine, and it will stay mine (I hope).

And in this small piece of the internet I have to myself, I am going to write random things that cross my mind.

It's a blog of sorts. But it's not really a blog. Is it? Perhaps it is. Maybe it's not.
